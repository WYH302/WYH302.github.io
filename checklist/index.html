Publishing checklist

Use this before sharing the site publicly

The draft site is safe because it avoids personal claims. Before turning it into a real academic homepage, replace the draft content with verified public information.

Identity

  • Add the real name, department, university, field, and location.
  • Add advisor information only if it is accurate and appropriate to publish.
  • Use a formal profile photo that you are comfortable publishing.
  • Add verified public email, GitHub, Scholar, ORCID, and LinkedIn links.

Academic content

  • Write specific research interests rather than broad slogans.
  • Describe projects with problem, method, result, and personal contribution.
  • Label publications, preprints, posters, and working papers honestly.
  • Publish blog notes only when they are complete and useful.

Safety

  • Do not commit secrets, credentials, private records, or identity documents.
  • Do not upload restricted papers, raw data, review comments, or internal lab materials.
  • Check image, figure, code, dataset, and PDF sharing permissions.
  • Run the local check and build commands before publishing.
