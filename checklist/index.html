Publishing checklist

Use this before sharing the site publicly

Use this checklist before adding more public academic content. Sensitive personal information, unverified publication status, and restricted project details should stay off the public website.

Identity

  • Keep only a professional name, affiliation, field, and public academic contact.
  • Publish advisor information only when it is accurate and appropriate.
  • Use a formal profile photo only after it is approved for public use.
  • Add verified public email, GitHub, Scholar, ORCID, and LinkedIn links.

Academic content

  • Write specific research interests rather than broad slogans.
  • Describe projects with problem, method, and public contribution only.
  • Separate accepted papers from ongoing manuscripts and preprints.
  • Publish blog notes only when they are complete and useful.

Safety

  • Do not publish phone numbers, birth dates, private addresses, secrets, or identity documents.
  • Do not upload restricted papers, raw data, review comments, internal lab materials, or unapproved project metrics.
  • Check image, figure, code, dataset, patent-status, and PDF sharing permissions.
  • Run the local check and build commands before publishing.
