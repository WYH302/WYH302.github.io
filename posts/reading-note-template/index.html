Research note template

This page is a writing scaffold, not a published research note. Replace it with a real paper reading note only after the note is complete and accurately cites the paper being discussed.

Suggested structure

  • Problem: What question does the paper or method address?
  • Main idea: What is the central mechanism or contribution?
  • Method and evidence: What data, model, proof, or evaluation supports the claim?
  • What I learned: How did the work change your understanding?
  • Limitations: What assumptions, baselines, or settings remain uncertain?

Before publishing

Add the real paper title, authors, venue, link, and your own analysis. Do not present an empty template as a dated blog post.
