Research note · 2026-06-19

Multimodal agents for computational imaging

This note outlines a public research direction: connecting multimodal perception, tool-using agents, and computational imaging systems. It is a research agenda note, not a claim of completed experimental results.

Motivation

Computational imaging systems often combine physical measurements, reconstruction algorithms, and downstream perception models. As multimodal foundation models and agentic workflows mature, a natural question is whether an intelligent system can reason about the sensing process itself: what to measure, how to reconstruct, which tools to call, and when the result is reliable enough for a downstream decision.

Research questions

  • How can multimodal models represent optical measurements, sparse observations, and reconstructed visual content in one reasoning loop?
  • How can agents use tools such as simulators, reconstruction modules, segmentation models, and evaluation scripts without hiding uncertainty?
  • How can a system decide when to request more measurements, change a sensing strategy, or defer a prediction?

Possible direction

One promising direction is to treat the imaging pipeline as an inspectable workflow. A multimodal agent can coordinate measurement selection, reconstruction, visual analysis, and reporting, while each step remains traceable. In this framing, the agent is not a black-box replacement for physics or signal processing. It is a controller and reasoning layer that helps connect physical priors, learned models, and human-facing explanations.

Practical caution

The main risk is overclaiming autonomy. A useful research prototype should report uncertainty, preserve intermediate artifacts, and separate model-generated suggestions from verified measurements. For public academic communication, method notes should distinguish proposals, ongoing work, and validated results.
